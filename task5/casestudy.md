# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI fraud is a problem in India. People who use UPI to pay for things can get tricked into sending money to bad people. These bad people usually target students, people who work and older people who use payment apps.

It usually starts when someone gets a call or a message that says it is from their bank or from a company that delivers things. The person on the phone might say that the user needs to check their account or get some money. Then they ask the user to click on a link or say yes to a payment request in their UPI app. Sometimes these bad people send a request that says they want to get money from the user. Really they are trying to take money from the user. When the user says yes the money goes from their account to the persons account right away.

These scams work because they make the user feel like they have to do something without thinking about it. People who are new to payments or do not know how UPI works are very likely to get tricked.

When someone gets tricked they can lose money away feel very stressed and have a hard time getting their money back. Sometimes users also share codes or personal details and that can lead to more problems, with their account.

This case shows how important it is to know how online payment systems work and to be very careful when someone asks you to do something that you do not know about. UPI fraud is a deal and people need to be aware of UPI fraud so they can protect themselves from UPI fraud.
